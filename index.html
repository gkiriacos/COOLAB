Election Map

The election map displays the differing percentages of Trump and Bidens victory margins with darker colors being he largest margin. When looking at the map at first glance you may think that there is a huge Republican majority, but due to the population density in various areas of Pennsylvania the difference is much smaller than you may realize highlighting the importance of voter engagement. Party officials or campaign managers should utilize this map in order to put more campaign funding and effort into areas that seem to have a lower percentage of support for candidates in order to attempt to sway those voters.

Dot Density Map

This map shows the percentage of Hispanic individuals by state with the lightest colors representing the highest percent. I chose a chloropleth map to clearly see which areas had the highest Hispanic percentage. This map could be used to analyze population trends in the US and encourage cities to diversify certain areas or protect ethnic enclaves present. This map is extremely useful for regional planning purposes, as government will be able to see who lives where, and what to make available for those people.

Chloropleth Map

This map shows the distribution of the Hispanic population where one dot equals 400 people. I chose dot density to be able to see the visual clusters of people in specific geographic regions. This allows viewers to better picture where large populations are and for city officials to add resources to those areas. For example, there may be a need for language services in heavily clustered areas helping officials with both policy and planning.
